Abstract

Social robot navigation in culturally diverse environments demands more than collision avoidance; it requires robots to understand and respect local social norms that govern human spatial behavior. Current approaches rely on generic Western social conventions and cloud-based proprietary models, limiting their applicability in non-Western contexts and resource-constrained settings.

We present CSSR-Nav, the first social robot navigation system that integrates empirically-derived African cultural norms into autonomous navigation. Based on a survey of 143 Rwandan respondents, we encode seven navigation-relevant cultural norms into a three-component architecture: (1) a LLaVA-based perception pipeline combining YOLOv8 detection at 15 Hz with vision-language social reasoning every 3 seconds, (2) a Rwandan Social Costmap Layer that translates cultural norms into navigation costs using Gaussian personal space models and directional preference fields, and (3) a Cultural Behavior Coordinator that triggers appropriate greetings (“Muraho!”) and respectful gestures.

Deployed on a SoftBank Pepper robot with an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, our system runs entirely at the edge without cloud dependencies. We design a comprehensive 10-condition ablation study to isolate the contribution of each cultural norm to navigation performance and social compliance.

Key Contributions

  • First integration of empirically-derived African cultural norms (from 143 Rwandan survey respondents) into autonomous social robot navigation
  • Three-component architecture combining LLaVA perception, a Rwandan Social Costmap Layer, and a Cultural Behavior Coordinator
  • Fully edge-deployed system on NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano — no cloud dependencies or proprietary APIs
  • Comprehensive 10-condition ablation study design to evaluate individual cultural norm contributions
  • Open-source ROS costmap_2d plugin that integrates with standard navigation stacks without algorithmic changes

System Architecture

Three integrated components for culturally-aware navigation

LLaVA Perception

Two-stage pipeline: YOLOv8 runs at 15 Hz for fast person detection, while LLaVA performs social reasoning every 3 seconds to identify groups, elders, and social interactions.

ByteTrack for robust person ID persistence

Social Costmap Layer

ROS costmap_2d plugin encoding 7 cultural norms as navigation costs: Gaussian personal space, lethal group interaction zones, directional preferences, and elder respect zones.

Integrates with standard move_base

Behavior Coordinator

State machine managing cultural behaviors: Kinyarwanda greetings (“Muraho!”), respectful bowing gestures, and velocity scaling near people.

States: IDLE → NAVIGATING → GREETING → YIELDING

Rwandan Cultural Norms

Seven navigation-relevant norms from a survey of 143 Rwandan respondents

Norm ID Cultural Norm Implementation
3-1 Maintain ≤1m personal space Gaussian cost (σi=0.5m intimate, σp=1.0m personal)
2-26 Don't walk between conversing people Lethal cost (254) in interaction zone
3-3 Pass behind groups, not in front Asymmetric costs: front ×1.5, back ×0.7
2-27 Don't walk ahead of elders 2m × 2m zone with cost 180 ahead of elder
2-11 Approach to greet (don't wave from afar) Approaching behavior activation
